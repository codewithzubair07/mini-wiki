# Niklas Luhmann

## Summary
Niklas Luhmann (1927–1998) was a German sociologist and one of the most prolific social theorists of the twentieth century. He is celebrated in knowledge-management circles for developing the **Zettelkasten** method — a physical slip-box of ~90,000 interlinked index cards that underpinned his extraordinary output of 70+ books and 400+ scholarly articles.

## Key Facts
- Developed the Zettelkasten slip-box method across roughly four decades of work
- Accumulated approximately 90,000 index cards, each holding a single atomic idea
- Used explicit cross-references between cards to create an emergent network of ideas rather than a rigid hierarchy
- His Zettelkasten is considered the closest pre-digital implementation of Vannevar Bush's Memex "associative trails" concept
- Primary field: sociology and social systems theory
